Make an informed decision

A comprehensive Cyber Security Assessment will give you detailed insight of the specific vulnerabilities your organisation is exposed to. It will identify and prioritise business risk and make independent and expert recommendations for risk mitigation, ensuring your business is cyber secure.

Our Cyber assessments are carried out against an agreed standard set of controls and covers technology, process, policy, physical and people aspects of Cyber Security. We will interview a number of stakeholders within the organisation, from the executive, through the IT function and, where required, down to a user level to ensure a diverse understanding of your business.

Once complete, any and all weaknesses will be highlighted in a comprehensive “Risk & Recommendations” report. This report will contain an executive level management summary, giving high level insight to the vulnerabilities we have identified, and the potential impact to the business should those vulnerabilities be exploited. In addition we will complete a detailed technical breakdown, for the IT function to consume, as well as prioritised recommendations for perusal and action.

Outcomes from your cyber security assessment
  • Identified Vulnerabilities

    Our assessment will throw a spotlight on the weaknesses and associated risks within your organisation.

  • Informed Investment Decisions

    By understanding the inherent Cyber risks you are exposed to, investment can be done in an informed and deliberate manner rather than in an ad-hoc manner.

  • Cyber Maturity

    Being able to prepare the business to identify, respond and remediate against cyber threats will increase the maturity of the organisation significantly.

  • Focused Action

    By understanding which areas to target first, this means a focus can be given for the best return of investment.

  • Cultural Change

    Cultural change is hard to achieve, but cost effective to deliver. Findings from our Cyber Assessments can contribute to cultural change towards being Security conscious.

  • Peace of Mind

    Knowing Cyber Security is a priority and being taken seriously can deliver comfort to employees, customers, shareholders and other stakeholders alike.

Steve Robinson
Chief Executive Officer

Hailing from a background in Operations, Littlefish CEO Steve Robinson is responsible for strategy and organisational development, as well as being heavily involved in meeting prospective customers and helping to shape the design of their service solutions.

Since acquiring Littlefish in 2010, Steve has overseen the business’s significant growth, marrying a laser-sharp focus on world-class service quality, with the creation of a profitable and sustainable business model. Steve has worked in the IT Industry since leaving school (sadly for Steve more than 20 years ago now), and prior to Littlefish, held senior roles in Computerland UK Plc and Capita Plc, where he was responsible for running a variety of services as well as helping to secure significant new business wins.

Outside of work Steve’s main preoccupation is with his young children, whose interests and hobbies have become his own – something he’s sure many parents will sympathise with! Being fortunate enough to live only a short walk from the City Ground, when Steve get’s the chance, he likes to follow his favourite football team, Nottingham Forest. He is also a keen(ish) runner and is currently training for the 2020 London Marathon. For the last few spare minutes of the day Steve likes to read, especially on subjects like current affairs, psychology, philosophy, politics and economics – which he often finds to be a good aid to a sound night’s sleep!
