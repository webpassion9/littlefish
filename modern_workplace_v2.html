The evolving modern workplace

The modern workplace is so much more than the devices we use and the functional assurance we now take for granted. The modern workplace is the starting point for business transformation. With data fast becoming an organisations biggest asset and with modern legislation making it a potential liability, the modern workplace focus is now about liberating, empowering, securing and leveraging your most expensive asset, your people.

With the modern workplace evolving at a rapid pace, today we need to support our users and ensure they are able to access their applications, services and data from any device, in any location, around the clock. By implementing the right end user strategy, the business benefit outweighs the investment tenfold as user productivity increases, user responsiveness grows, user well-being is enhanced and cost savings are achieved promptly. Not to mention the direct and indirect butterfly effect on the service being delivered to your clients.

Modern workplace solutions

In the modern workplace, our users want to be connected and have uninterrupted access to everything they need, when they need it. They want to blend work and play and access personal apps as well as continuing to perform their work-related tasks via work related tools. At the same time, they want to be safe in the knowledge that they are secure from the external threats, are supported by their IT Teams and have access to assistance instantly. By engaging Littlefish and drawing on our extensive experience, we will help you create the right end user computing strategy and practical application, encompassing some, or all, of the following elements:

  • EUC Strategy
  • EUC Architecture - Continued development of EUC model
  • Procurement
  • Device Deployment
  • Application Packaging
  • Release Deployment
  • Identity and access management - Active Directory based
  • Patch Management (Line Of Business Applications - SAP/Oracle)
  • End Point Protection Management - (Anti-Virus, Anti Malware, AntiSpam)
  • Desk Side Support
  • Image and Build Management - Device
  • Specific
  • Mobile Device Management
  • IT Asset disposition (ITAD)
  • Encryption Management
  • Training Materials
  • VIP Support
  • Application Testing
  • Compliance
Application Management

At its core, EUC is all about giving end-users the power to utilise modern technology to its full potential without the need for specialised technical knowledge. The key to doing this is to find the right software and make it accessible to everyone across the business.

At Littlefish, we take care of the whole application management process from the application packaging, procurement and deployment to testing, training and Cybersecurity. We identify old software and offer powerful modern software tools to introduce instead. The final goal is a streamlined working process that helps your business grow.

Mobile Device Management

To do any job right you need the right tools to do it and working in the modern workplace is no different. Digitalisation and connectivity offer limitless possibilities for innovation, better efficiency and higher productivity but to achieve that your team has to have constant access to the systems and applications that allow them to work remotely.

Your current tech, however, may not be up to the task as old hardware may no longer be equipped to support the latest software. This is why our end-user computing team will work with you to find the best way to obtain and introduce new devices within the limits of your budget.

End user compute Strategy

Switching to an end-user computing model is a huge transformation for any business. This is why it’s very important to have a clearly defined plan of how to introduce the new model successfully. With over 100 years of combined experience under their belt, our team of experts are here to design a EUC strategy that ensures the transition is easy and achieves the desired result.

We take the time to identify the roles and responsibilities within your organisation, as well as the technological demands of your workplace and any potential risks involved. We then find the right software and hardware solutions and we lay out the roadmap for their implementation.

Supporting The Needs of a Dynamic Workforce

Creating a dynamic workforce of employees who work remotely from multiple locations and devices while staying fully connected and perfectly in sync is the working model of the future and our end-user computing services are designed to support all its needs and requirements.

We help you build a reliable and secure communications network that keeps all your workers connected and allows for seamless information sharing in real time. We make sure that your team members have everything they need to interact with each other from anywhere in the world without any issues.

Steve Robinson
Chief Executive Officer

Hailing from a background in Operations, Littlefish CEO Steve Robinson is responsible for strategy and organisational development, as well as being heavily involved in meeting prospective customers and helping to shape the design of their service solutions.

Since acquiring Littlefish in 2010, Steve has overseen the business’s significant growth, marrying a laser-sharp focus on world-class service quality, with the creation of a profitable and sustainable business model. Steve has worked in the IT Industry since leaving school (sadly for Steve more than 20 years ago now), and prior to Littlefish, held senior roles in Computerland UK Plc and Capita Plc, where he was responsible for running a variety of services as well as helping to secure significant new business wins.

Outside of work Steve’s main preoccupation is with his young children, whose interests and hobbies have become his own – something he’s sure many parents will sympathise with! Being fortunate enough to live only a short walk from the City Ground, when Steve get’s the chance, he likes to follow his favourite football team, Nottingham Forest. He is also a keen(ish) runner and is currently training for the 2020 London Marathon. For the last few spare minutes of the day Steve likes to read, especially on subjects like current affairs, psychology, philosophy, politics and economics – which he often finds to be a good aid to a sound night’s sleep!
